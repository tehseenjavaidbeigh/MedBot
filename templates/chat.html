I've been having a persistent headache for 3 days, mild fever around 99.5°F, and some stiffness in my neck. Should I be concerned?
10:32 AM
Rx
The combination of symptoms you've described — persistent headache, low-grade fever, and neck stiffness — warrants prompt medical evaluation. While these can have benign causes like tension headache or mild viral illness, together they can sometimes indicate conditions that require professional assessment.
Possible Causes
🔵 Tension-type headache — Most common; usually stress/posture-related
🟡 Viral illness — Flu or respiratory infection with muscular tension
🔴 Meningeal irritation — Rare but serious; requires urgent evaluation
⚠️ The neck stiffness combined with fever is a symptom pattern your doctor should evaluate in person — please seek medical care within the next few hours if symptoms worsen.
